Plants & Remedies

The Leaves of our tree represent growth, healing, and everyday care. Here you’ll find guides to plants and their traditional uses, alongside modern remedies and wellness practices. From herbal teas to simple plant care tips, this page connects ancestral wisdom with practical knowledge for today.

Plant Growth Remedies Images
Aloe Vera To grow aloe vera, plant it in well-draining soil in a pot with a drainage hole, like a cactus mix, and place it in a bright location with direct sunlight. Water the plant only when the soil is completely dry, allowing excess water to drain away to prevent root rot. Soothes burns & skin irritation
Mint To grow mint, plant cuttings or established plants in pots to control its invasive spread, as it thrives in containers with well-draining soil. Mint needs consistent moisture, so water regularly, but avoid overwatering. Provide it with a partly shady spot, especially in hot climates, to prevent scorched leaves. Relieves digestive discomfort
Lavender To grow lavender successfully, plant it in a location with full sun and well-draining soil that is not overly fertile. Water deeply but infrequently, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings to prevent root rot. Calming scent, aids relaxation
Chamomile To grow chamomile, sow tiny seeds on the soil surface in a sunny location, providing moisture but no covering to allow light for germination, and transplant them after the last frost. Chamomile prefers full sun, tolerates various soils, and needs regular watering when young but becomes drought-tolerant once established. calming tea for stress and sleep.
Rosemary To grow rosemary, choose a sunny spot and well-draining soil, as the plant hates wet roots. Start with cuttings from a mature plant, rooting them in water or sandy potting mix. Plant them in the spring after the last frost, ensuring they receive 6-8 hours of sunlight daily. Water regularly but allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings. boosts memory, relieves headaches.
Turmeric To grow turmeric, prepare 1–3" rhizome pieces with buds, then let them dry for a day or two to prevent rot. Plant the pieces in well-draining, fertile soil (rich in compost) with the buds pointing upwards, 2–4" deep, and water well. Turmeric needs full sun or partial shade and consistently moist, but not wet, soil to thrive. anti-inflammatory, supports joint health.
